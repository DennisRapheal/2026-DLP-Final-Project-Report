	\begin{column}{2\colwidth+\sepwidth}
	\begin{block}{Project Overview}
		
		This project studies residual action correction for a frozen OpenPI-Comet
		Vision-Language-Action (VLA) policy on BEHAVIOR-1K. OpenPI-Comet predicts
		32-step continuous action chunks for long-horizon household manipulation.
		Instead of retraining the full VLA model, we train an Asynchronous Action
		Chunk Correction (A2C2) head that predicts a 23-dimensional residual for the
		selected base action at execution time.
		
		\blankfigure{20}{3.4}
		
		The target task is Task 18, \textit{Tidying Bedroom}. It is selected because
		the OpenPI-Comet validation ACC prior shows non-saturated baseline competence:
		Task 1 is already saturated, while Task 18 is the next assigned task with
		non-zero reported capability. This gives the correction module measurable
		headroom while avoiding tasks where the base policy appears to have no initial
		competence.
		
	\end{block}
	\end{column}

	\end{columns}

	\begin{columns}[t]
		\separatorcolumn
		
		\begin{column}{\colwidth}
			
			\begin{thm}{Research Question}{}
				
				Can a small online residual head improve a frozen action-chunking VLA policy
				without retraining the base model?
				
				For source frame $t$, OpenPI-Comet predicts a base action chunk
				$A^b_t = \{a^b_{t,0}, \ldots, a^b_{t,H-1}\}$ with horizon $H=32$ and
				action dimension $d_a=23$. For execution offset $k$, A2C2 predicts:
				$$
				a^{exec}_{t+k} = a^b_{t,k} + \lambda \Delta a_{t,k}.
				$$
				
				The core hypothesis is that the latest execution observation can correct
				stale or locally invalid actions inside a long action chunk while preserving
				the base policy's high-level intent.
				
			\end{thm}
			
			\begin{alertblock}{Task Selection}
				
				We filter tasks based on their validation ACC prior. Task 18 is chosen as the
				main target because it balances base-policy competence and remaining
				improvement potential.
				
				\begin{table}
					\centering
					\begin{tabular}{l l r c}
						\toprule
						\textbf{ID} & \textbf{Task} & \textbf{ACC Prior} & \textbf{Avg Frames} \\
						\midrule
						1 & Picking Up Trash & 100\% & 5268 \\
						7 & Picking Up Toys & 0\% & 18891 \\
						18 & Tidying Bedroom & 20\% & 11037 \\
						\bottomrule
					\end{tabular}
					\caption{OpenPI-Comet validation ACC prior used only for task selection.}
				\end{table}
				
			\end{alertblock}
			
			\begin{defbox}{Training Signal}{}
				
				Each training example aligns a source-frame base chunk with a target-frame
				expert demonstration action. The residual target is:
				$$
				\Delta a^*_{t,k} = a^*_{t+k} - a^b_{t,k}.
				$$
				
				The correction head is trained with residual mean-squared error:
				$$
				L(\theta) = \frac{1}{N}\sum_i \|g_\theta(x_i)-\Delta a^*_i\|_2^2.
				$$
				
				Valid-entry masks remove chunk offsets that cross episode boundaries. The
				base policy remains frozen throughout training and online evaluation.
				
			\end{defbox}
			
			\begin{block}{Correction Head Inputs}
				
				The implemented A2C2 head is a multimodal token-fusion model rather than a
				state-only MLP.
				
				\begin{itemize}
					\item \textbf{Source-frame context:} cached action chunk, selected action,
					valid mask, OpenPI-Comet prefix latent $z_t$, and inference timing.
					\item \textbf{Execution-frame context:} proprioception, RGB-D observations,
					task language, camera-relative pose, and task-info features.
					\item \textbf{Model structure:} token projections, language GRU, frozen
					ResNet-18 visual encoders, six-layer Transformer encoder, and residual MLP.
				\end{itemize}
				
				\blankfigure{13}{3}
				
			\end{block}
			
		\end{column}
	
		\separatorcolumn
		
		\begin{column}{\colwidth}
			
			\begin{block}{Training and Online Evaluation}
				
				The residual head is trained with AdamW using learning rate $10^{-5}$,
				weight decay $10^{-5}$, gradient clipping at 10, validation split 0.05,
				and a 400,000-step training budget. Offline validation reports residual
				MSE/MAE, corrected-action MSE, and base-action MSE.
				
				Online evaluation uses a receding-horizon wrapper. At each source frame,
				OpenPI-Comet predicts a 32-step chunk and caches the COMET prefix latent.
				At every execution step, A2C2 reads the latest RGB-D/proprioceptive state,
				selects the current base action, predicts a residual, and returns:
				$$
				\text{selected\_base\_action} + \text{correction\_scale} \cdot \text{residual}.
				$$
				
			\end{block}
			
			\begin{exampleblock}{Results}{}
				
				Fixed-seed OpenPI-Comet baseline rollouts over ten instances show that Task
				18 has partial but limited progress, making it a meaningful correction target.
				
				\begin{table}
					\centering
					\begin{tabular}{l r r c}
						\toprule
						\textbf{ID} & \textbf{Task} & \textbf{Mean $q_{final}$} & \textbf{SD} \\
						\midrule
						1 & Picking Up Trash & 0.8333 & 0.2236 \\
						7 & Picking Up Toys & 0.1833 & 0.2291 \\
						18 & Tidying Bedroom & 0.1333 & 0.2211 \\
						21 & Children's Toys & 0.4667 & 0.2332 \\
						27 & Sorting Items & 0.1250 & 0.1768 \\
						29 & Clean Up Desk & 0.0909 & 0.0707 \\
						\bottomrule
					\end{tabular}
					\caption{Local fixed-seed baseline results over ten instances per assigned task.}
				\end{table}
				
				For Task 18 online comparison, the corrected policy performs worse than the
				frozen baseline:
				$$
				\text{mean } q_{final}: \quad 0.1333 \rightarrow 0.0000.
				$$
				The baseline achieves nonzero progress on 3/10 IDs; A2C2 achieves 0/10.
				
			\end{exampleblock}

			\begin{thm}{Potential Risk: Random Chunk Supervision Can Break Mode Consistency}{}
				
				The base flow policy samples a temporally coherent action chunk:
				$$
				A^b_t \sim \pi_b(A_t \mid o_t, \ell).
				$$
				However, A2C2 is trained with randomly sampled chunk offsets. For an offset
				$k$, the residual target is defined locally as
				$$
				\Delta a^{target}_{t,k}
				=
				a^{expert}_{t+k} - a^b_{t,k}.
				$$

				This provides only local one-step supervision. If multiple valid chunks exist
				under similar observations, the sampled expert action may belong to a different
				mode from the base action chunk. Then the residual head is trained to partially
				pull a coherent base chunk toward a locally different expert mode:
				$$
				a^b_{t,k} + \Delta a_\phi
				\approx
				a^{expert}_{t+k}.
				$$

				As a result, the correction may not collapse an entire trajectory, but it can
				break chunk-level mode consistency. The corrected action sequence may mix
				parts of different modes, reducing the temporal coherence originally produced
				by the flow-based base policy.
				
			\end{thm}
			
			\begin{block}{Discussion}
				
				The result is negative but informative. The implementation matches the A2C2
				formulation: it keeps OpenPI-Comet frozen, trains expert-minus-base
				residuals, uses latest execution-frame RGB-D/proprioception/language, and
				deploys the head inside an online receding-horizon control loop.
				
				\begin{itemize}
					\item \textbf{Offline-online mismatch:} training states come from expert
					demonstrations, but online rollouts visit policy-induced states with missed
					grasps, contact errors, and shifted object poses.
					\item \textbf{Action-scale imbalance:} raw MSE may be dominated by large arm
					coordinates while small gripper/contact dimensions remain task-critical.
					\item \textbf{Overcorrection:} an unconservative residual can perturb a base
					policy that was already partially competent.
				\end{itemize}
				
				\blankfigure{13}{3}
				
			\end{block}
			
			\begin{block}{Conclusion and Next Steps}
				A2C2 does not improve Task 18 under the current configuration, reducing mean
				$q_{final}$ from 0.1333 to 0.0000. The next iteration should focus on making
				the residual module more conservative and better matched to online states.
				
				\begin{itemize}
					\item Normalize or reweight the loss by action dimension.
					\item Ablate correction scales such as 0.25, 0.5, and 1.0.
					\item Add residual norm clipping and an uncertainty gate that can keep the
					base action unchanged.
					\item Analyze performance by chunk offset to separate stale-chunk errors from
					missing high-level capability.
					\item Add online data, rollout-state relabeling, or hard-negative states.
				\end{itemize}
			\end{block}
